The Arctic

Facts about the Arctic

The Artic is the region that surronds the North Pole. With most of its landmass being comprised of Tundra and permaforst almost all of its flora are mosses, lichen, and bushes. The Arctic is under constant threat of overexploitation from oil, fishing, and mining companies. During the summer and winter regions the sun either does not set, with the summer having sun at all hours and the winter being in total darkness

Countries that are inside the Arctic

  2. Finland
  3. Sweden
  4. Canada
  5. Greenland

Facts about Arctic Animals

The presence of bears in the Arctic is how we got its name as the word "Arctic" is deprived from the greek word "bear" and "Antarctica" means "no bears". Despite its harsh climate the Arctic there are over 21,000 known species that call it home. Despite common belief pengiuns do not live in the Arctic, they instead live in the Southern Hemisphere. Inside the arctic regions of Canada the only people allowed to hunt narwhals are certain Inuit communities.
